English Learning for Beginners: At the Pharmacy

Dialogue

Alice: Oh, Bob! Is that you? I didn't expect to see you here!

Bob: Alice! My hero! Yes, it's me. I'm on a quest for the legendary 'Cold Slayer' medicine. My nose feels like a leaky faucet, and my head is a drum solo.

Alice: A 'Cold Slayer'? Are you sure it's not just a regular cold? You always get so dramatic.

Bob: Dramatic? This is serious, Alice! I need something for my sore throat, too. It feels like I swallowed a cactus.

Alice: Okay, okay. Let's find something. Have you asked the pharmacist for advice?

Bob: Not yet. I was admiring the colorful bandages. Do you think a glittery one would make me feel better?

Alice: Probably not for a sore throat, Bob. Look, there's the pharmacist. Let's go ask her.

Bob: But what should I say? My voice is so hoarse!

Alice: Just tell her your symptoms. "Hello, I have a sore throat and a runny nose." Easy.

Bob: Right. "Hello, kind dispenser of healing elixirs! My nasal passages are in rebellion and my vocal cords are staging a protest!"

Alice: (whispering) Bob, just "Hello, I have a sore throat and a runny nose."

Pharmacist: Can I help you?

Bob: (clears throat dramatically) Yes, I have a sore throat and a runny nose, and my head...

Alice: (interjecting smoothly) And he's looking for some medicine for a common cold. Something for the throat and nose.

Pharmacist: I see. We have a good syrup for sore throats and nasal sprays for runny noses. Would you like a pain reliever for your head too?

Bob: A pain reliever! Yes! My head is a rock concert!

Alice: (to Bob) Okay, let's get the syrup, the spray, and some pain relievers. You'll be fine.

Bob: Thank you, Alice. You saved my life. Or at least my weekend.

Alice: You're welcome. And next time, maybe just a cough drop for your 'cactus throat'.

Current Situation

A pharmacy, sometimes called a drugstore, is a place where you can buy medicines. There are two main types of medicines: 'prescription medicines' (which you need a doctor's note for) and 'over-the-counter' medicines (which you can buy freely). At a pharmacy, you can talk to a pharmacist, who is a trained professional who can give you advice about medicines and minor health issues. Besides medicine, many pharmacies also sell things like first-aid supplies (bandages, antiseptic), toiletries (soap, shampoo), and even snacks or drinks. When you're not feeling well, the pharmacy is a great place to get help for common problems like a cold, a headache, or a small cut.

Key Phrases

  • sore throat: I have a sore throat. It hurts when I swallow.
  • runny nose: My nose is dripping, I have a runny nose.
  • headache: I have a headache, my head hurts.
  • common cold: I think I have a common cold.
  • medicine: I need some medicine for my cough.
  • pharmacist: Can I talk to the pharmacist, please?
  • syrup: This cough syrup tastes sweet.
  • nasal spray: Use this nasal spray for your blocked nose.
  • pain reliever: Do you have any pain reliever for back pain?
  • prescription: This medicine requires a prescription from a doctor.
  • over-the-counter: You can buy this over-the-counter, no prescription needed.
  • symptoms: What are your symptoms?

Grammar Points

  1. "I have..." for symptoms:

    This is a very common and easy way to talk about what you are feeling or what problem you have.

    • Structure: I have + a/an + symptom.
    • Examples: "I have a sore throat." "I have a headache." "I have a runny nose."

  2. Asking for things: "Can I get...?" / "Do you have...?"

    When you are at a shop or pharmacy and want to ask for something politely.

    • Structure: Can I get + (some/a/an) + item? or Do you have + (any/some) + item?
    • Examples: "Can I get some cough syrup?" "Do you have any pain reliever?"

  3. Basic Questions: "What are your symptoms?" / "Can I help you?"

    These are common questions you might hear or use.

    • "What are your symptoms?" is how a doctor or pharmacist asks about your problems.
    • "Can I help you?" is a polite offer of assistance.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 1: Fill in the Blanks

Choose the best word to complete the sentences.

(pharmacist, sore throat, medicine, runny nose, headache)

  1. I have a terrible __________. My head really hurts.
  2. My nose is dripping. I have a __________.
  3. The __________ can give you advice about medicines.
  4. I need some __________ for my cough.
  5. It hurts when I swallow because I have a __________.

Answers:

  1. headache
  2. runny nose
  3. pharmacist
  4. medicine
  5. sore throat

Exercise 2: Match the Symptom to the Solution

Match the health problem on the left with the item you might need on the right.

  1. Sore throat a. Nasal spray
  2. Runny nose b. Pain reliever
  3. Headache c. Cough syrup

Answers:

  1. Sore throat - c. Cough syrup
  2. Runny nose - a. Nasal spray
  3. Headache - b. Pain reliever

Exercise 3: Complete the Dialogue

Complete the short dialogue using words from the box.

(symptoms, help, sore throat, medicine, have)

Pharmacist: Hello, can I __________ you?

Customer: Yes, please. I __________ a __________. (Choose two words for this sentence)

Pharmacist: I see. What other __________ do you have?

Customer: Just a sore throat. Do you have any good __________ for it?

Pharmacist: Yes, we have a great syrup.

Answers:

Pharmacist: Hello, can I help you?

Customer: Yes, please. I have a sore throat.

Pharmacist: I see. What other symptoms do you have?

Customer: Just a sore throat. Do you have any good medicine for it?

Pharmacist: Yes, we have a great syrup.
